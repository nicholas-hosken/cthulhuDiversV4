As the name suggests, not so long ago, Cenote Carwash, based rooughly 2 hours away from Cancun, was used by local taxi drivers to literally wash their cars. Don't let this put you off visiting though, as this cenote as it is still considered one of the most beautiful underwater caves in the world and is considered a bucket-list destination for avid cave and cavern diving enthusiasts. Luckily it remains unspoiled despite past human intervention and this extraordinary cenote continues to mesmerizes our divers with its translucent waters and captivating geological formations.

Nestled near Tulum, Mexico, on the southern edge of the Yucatan Peninsula, Carwash boasts a large entrance with a diameter of approximately 165ft or 50m. For cavern divers, a max depth of 52ft or 15m is possible but, for certified Full Cave Divers, depths of 115ft or 35m can be reached during your penetration. The cenote's verdant surroundings harmoniously embrace delicate rock formations, crafted over countless millennia.

The surface of the water is often covered by an algae bloom veil during the warmer months of the year. Hidden beneath, the crystal clear water houses entrances to numerous caverns and caves containing some of the most spectacular underwater rock formations you'll ever see. In particular the White Room, the Room of Tears and the Chamber of Ancients make for some amazing underwater photography.

The unparalleled visibility within the cenote also ensures a clear view of the extraordinary underwater landscape and delicate rock formations which have been crafted over countless millennia. Exploring the cenote's caverns, tunnels, and lush vegetation guarantees an enthralling and indelible diving experience you simply won't find in other parts of the world.

Divers embarking on an exploration of the Carwash Cenote can anticipate encountering an extraordinary underwater realm, replete with caverns, tunnels, and thriving cenote vegetation. Remarkable visibility and a comfortable water temperature of around 77°F 25°C enhance the overall experience. Essential safety considerations include maintaining proper buoyancy control and adhering to environmental conservation practices which your dive guide will go over with you on route to the site.

As far a cenotes go, the Carwash Cenote sustains a rich diversity of marine life, including various species of fish, turtles, small crocs and other fascinating aquatic creatures. The cenote's flora is equally captivating, playing an integral role within its delicate ecosystem an offering some unique opportunities for photographers.

Diving in the Carwash Cenote requires divers to possess an Open Water certification or higher. Given the cenote's unique environment, previous diving experience is strongly recommended. Crucial safety guidelines for diving in the Carwash Cenote encompass meticulous equipment checks, the buddy system, and precise buoyancy control. Additionally, it is imperative to demonstrate reverence for the cenote's delicate ecosystem and practice environmental conservation.

In addition to scuba diving, this natural sinkhole offers excellent opportunities for swimming and snorkeling as it boasts some clear, refreshing waters where encounters with local wildlife, such as turtles and fish, is not uncommon. Located just 8 kilometers from Tulum in the Riviera Maya, this destination is not only easily accessible but also a hidden gem perfect for those looking to enjoy watery pursuits in a serene setting.

Frequently Asked Questions:

What level of visibility can be expected within the cenote?
The Carwash Cenote offers exceptional visibility, affording divers a clear and unobstructed view of the remarkable underwater landscape. Expect 150ft or 50m of visibility once below the algae bloom.

Can novice divers explore the Carwash Cenote?
Yes, exploration of the Carwash Cenote only requires divers to hold at least an Open Water certification. However this cenote is better suited to professional photographers who are looking for unique photo opportunities and aren’t concerned about spending a lot of time in an overhead environment. For first time cenote divers, we would strongly suggest that you consider either Chac Mool or Tajma Ha as both of these options offer more time spent underground in the caverns.

Are there any age restrictions for diving in the cenote?
Technically there are no specific age restrictions for diving in the Carwash Cenote. Nevertheless, for safety considerations, Cthulhu Divers management has stipulated a minimum age requirement of 15 for our cenote tours.

What equipment is necessary for diving in the cenote?
On top of standard diving equipment, each diver must have a torch. Wetsuits are also strongly recommend. Cthulhu Divers includes all necessary equipment rental in our pricing. Please note that snorkels, knives, gloves and SMB's are not allowed into any of the caverns.

Are there restroom facilities and changing areas near the cenote?
Restroom facilities and changing areas are conveniently available in close proximity to the cenote.

Is a tour guide required or can divers explore independently?
Divers with a Full Cave Diver certification can apply directly with the cenote for permission to dive solo. However this permission is rarely granted. Other divers without this certification will need a dive guide with the following qualifications:

  • A PADI (or equivalent) Professional certification (Divemaster or higher)
  • A TDI (or equivalent) Full Cave Diver Certification
  • A license from the specific cenote, in this case Carwash, owners which grants them permission to lead dives in the cenote.

The Carwash Cenote offers a truly exceptional and unforgettable scuba diving encounter, showcasing its mesmerizingly clear waters and awe-inspiring geological formations. Adhering to essential safety guidelines and exhibiting profound respect for the cenote's delicate ecosystem ensures a secure and memorable dive adventure.
